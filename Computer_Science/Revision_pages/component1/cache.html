Cache - In depth

Speeds up CPU by allowing faster access to data and instructions

Types of cache

Level 1 - Level 1 cache is the smallest and fastest cache because it is closest to the CPU. In many cases, modern CPUs contain Level 1 cache. Each CPU core will have its own Level 1 cache.

Level 2 - Level 2 cache is slightly slower and larger than Level 1 cache. It is further away from the CPU and thus it takes longer to retrieve instructions and data. Cores in a CPU will usually have to share the Level 2 cache.

Level 3 - Level 3 cache is the largest type of cache which is located furthest away from the CPU. All cores must share this cache.
